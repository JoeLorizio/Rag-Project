2. EMPLOYMENT POLICIES
================================================================================

2.1 EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY
----------------------------------
Nexus Technology Solutions is an equal opportunity employer. We do not
discriminate in hiring, promotion, compensation, termination, or any other
employment condition on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, national
origin, age, disability, veteran status, sexual orientation, gender identity,
or any other characteristic protected by applicable law.

2.2 AT-WILL EMPLOYMENT
-----------------------
Employment at Nexus Technology Solutions is at-will. This means that either
the employee or the company may terminate the employment relationship at any
time, with or without cause, and with or without advance notice, subject to
the terms of any written employment agreement in effect.

Nothing in this handbook creates a contract of employment or guarantees
employment for any specific period.

2.3 BACKGROUND CHECKS
----------------------
All offers of employment are conditional upon successful completion of a
background check, including criminal history and employment verification.
Certain roles requiring access to financial data, customer PII, or government
systems may require additional screening including credit history checks.

2.4 EMPLOYMENT ELIGIBILITY
---------------------------
All employees must be legally authorized to work in the United States. I-9
verification is completed on the first day of employment. Nexus uses E-Verify
for all new hires.

2.5 PROBATIONARY PERIOD
------------------------
All new employees serve a 90-day introductory period. During this period,
new employees are not eligible for certain benefits (see Section 4). The
introductory period is a time for the employee and manager to assess fit.
At-will employment status applies throughout and after the introductory period.

3. COMPENSATION AND PAY
================================================================================

3.1 PAY SCHEDULE
-----------------
Nexus Technology Solutions pays employees on a bi-weekly schedule, every
other Friday. The pay period runs Sunday through Saturday. In the event a
payday falls on a holiday, payroll will be processed on the preceding business
day.

All employees are paid via direct deposit. Paper checks are not available.
Employees must provide banking information to HR within their first 3 business
days. Payroll is processed through ADP Workforce Now.

3.2 SALARY BANDS
-----------------
Nexus maintains compensation bands for all roles, reviewed annually in Q4.
Bands are calibrated against Radford and Mercer technology industry survey
data, targeting the 60th percentile for base compensation. Total compensation
(including bonus and benefits) targets the 65th percentile.

Employees may request a copy of their current band from HR. Salary discussions
between colleagues are permitted and protected by the National Labor Relations
Act.

3.3 PERFORMANCE BONUSES
------------------------
Annual performance bonuses are paid in February based on individual and
company performance for the prior fiscal year. The bonus structure is:

Individual Contributors: Up to 10% of annual base salary
Senior / Principal level: Up to 15% of annual base salary
Management: Up to 20% of annual base salary
VP level: Up to 30% of annual base salary

Bonus payouts are at the discretion of management and are not guaranteed.
Employees must be employed on the bonus payment date to be eligible. New hires
who join after October 1 are not eligible for the current year bonus cycle.

3.4 ON-CALL COMPENSATION
-------------------------
Employees participating in on-call rotations receive the following additional
compensation:

On-call stipend: $200/week for each week on primary on-call rotation
Incident response compensation: $75/hour for hours worked outside of normal
business hours (7am-7pm CT M-F) during a declared P1 or P2 incident

On-call stipends are paid monthly with the regular payroll. Incident response
hours are logged in ServiceNow and approved by the manager before processing.

3.5 OVERTIME
-------------
Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime pay at 1.5x their regular
hourly rate for hours worked in excess of 40 hours in a workweek. Overtime
must be pre-approved by the direct manager except in cases of declared P1
incidents, where overtime is automatically approved.

All engineering and infrastructure positions at Nexus are classified as
exempt under the FLSA. If you believe your classification may be incorrect,
contact HR.

4. BENEFITS
================================================================================

4.1 HEALTH INSURANCE
---------------------
Nexus Technology Solutions offers three health insurance plan options through
BlueCross BlueShield of Illinois, effective the first of the month following
30 days of employment.

PLAN 1 — NEXUS PLUS (PPO)
- Deductible: $500 individual / $1,000 family
- Out-of-pocket maximum: $3,000 individual / $6,000 family
- In-network co-pay: $20 primary care / $40 specialist
- Prescription coverage: $10 generic / $30 brand / $60 specialty
- Employee monthly premium: $145 (individual) / $310 (employee + spouse)
  $285 (employee + children) / $420 (family)
- Nexus contribution: 82% of total premium

PLAN 2 — NEXUS SELECT (HDHP + HSA)
- Deductible: $1,500 individual / $3,000 family
- Out-of-pocket maximum: $4,500 individual / $9,000 family
- HSA contributions: Nexus contributes $800/year (individual) or $1,600/year
  (family) to your HSA account
- Employee monthly premium: $65 (individual) / $140 (employee + spouse)
  $120 (employee + children) / $190 (family)
- Nexus contribution: 88% of total premium
- This plan is compatible with a Health Savings Account (HSA)

PLAN 3 — NEXUS CORE (HMO)
- Deductible: $250 individual / $500 family
- Out-of-pocket maximum: $2,500 individual / $5,000 family
- Primary care co-pay: $10
- Referrals required for specialist visits
- No out-of-network coverage except emergencies
- Employee monthly premium: $95 (individual) / $215 (employee + spouse)
  $185 (employee + children) / $310 (family)
- Nexus contribution: 85% of total premium

THREE WAYS TO SAVE MONEY WITH YOUR HEALTHCARE BENEFITS:

1. CHOOSE THE HDHP + HSA PLAN (Plan 2) IF YOU ARE GENERALLY HEALTHY
   The Nexus Select plan has significantly lower premiums than Plan 1.
   Combined with the $800 annual HSA contribution from Nexus, healthy
   employees who do not frequently visit doctors often save $1,000 or more
   annually compared to the PPO. HSA funds roll over year to year and can
   be invested for long-term healthcare savings.

2. USE IN-NETWORK PROVIDERS
   All three plans offer significantly reduced costs when using in-network
   providers. BlueCross BlueShield has one of the largest provider networks
   in Illinois. Using an out-of-network provider on Plans 1 or 2 can result
   in 40-60% higher cost-sharing. Use the BCBS provider finder at
   bcbsil.com/find-a-doctor before scheduling appointments.

3. USE THE EMPLOYEE ASSISTANCE PROGRAM (EAP)
   The EAP is completely FREE and provides unlimited telephonic mental health
   counseling, up to 8 free in-person therapy sessions per year, financial
   counseling, legal consultations, and referrals. Many employees use
   expensive specialist visits for mental health when the EAP covers the same
   services at no cost. Call 1-800-555-0199 or visit eap.nexustech.io.

5. TIME OFF AND LEAVE POLICIES
================================================================================

5.1 PAID TIME OFF (PTO)
------------------------
Nexus uses a combined PTO policy for vacation, personal days, and sick time.
PTO begins accruing on the first day of employment.

Accrual schedule:
Years 0-2: 15 days (120 hours) per year / 4.62 hours per pay period
Years 3-5: 20 days (160 hours) per year / 6.15 hours per pay period
Years 6+: 25 days (200 hours) per year / 7.69 hours per pay period

Maximum PTO balance: 1.5x annual accrual rate
(Example: Engineer in year 1 can carry max 180 hours = 22.5 days)

PTO beyond the maximum is forfeited. Employees approaching the cap will be
notified by HR. Managers are expected to approve PTO requests to help
employees avoid forfeiture.

On-call engineers: PTO may be taken during on-call weeks, but the engineer
remains responsible for on-call response. If a P1 occurs during PTO, the
IC may request secondary on-call to take over at their discretion.

5.2 HOLIDAYS
-------------
Nexus observes the following paid holidays in 2026:

New Year's Day — January 1
Martin Luther King Jr. Day — January 19
Memorial Day — May 25
Independence Day — July 4 (observed July 3, Friday)
Labor Day — September 7
Thanksgiving Day — November 26
Day After Thanksgiving — November 27
Christmas Eve — December 24
Christmas Day — December 25

If a holiday falls on a weekend, the preceding Friday or following Monday
is observed. Holiday schedules are published annually in December.

NOC staff working on holidays receive 1.5x pay for hours worked on the
holiday (regardless of exempt/non-exempt status for all NOC roles).

5.3 SICK LEAVE
---------------
Sick leave is included in the PTO accrual above. There is no separate sick
bucket. Employees are not required to find coverage for sick days, but should
notify their manager and any on-call schedule administrators as soon as
possible.

Absences of 3 or more consecutive days may require a physician's note.
Extended illness may trigger short-term disability benefits (see Section 4.4).

5.4 PARENTAL LEAVE
-------------------
BIRTH PARENT LEAVE: 16 weeks fully paid, beginning at birth or adoption
NON-BIRTH PARENT LEAVE: 8 weeks fully paid, within 12 months of birth or
adoption placement

Parental leave is separate from and in addition to PTO. It may be taken
consecutively or intermittently within 12 months of birth/adoption.

Employees must notify HR and their manager at least 30 days in advance when
possible. Nexus does not require employees to exhaust PTO before using
parental leave.

5.5 BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
----------------------
Employees receive paid bereavement leave as follows:
- Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 days paid
- Extended family (grandparents, in-laws, aunt, uncle): 3 days paid
- Close friend or colleague: 1 day paid (manager approval)
Additional unpaid leave may be approved at manager discretion.

5.6 JURY DUTY AND MILITARY LEAVE
----------------------------------
Jury Duty: Nexus pays the difference between jury pay and regular salary for
up to 90 days of jury service.

Military Leave: Nexus complies fully with USERRA. Employees called to active
military duty receive salary continuation for up to 2 weeks per year. For
extended military leave, Nexus supplements the difference between military
pay and civilian salary for up to 6 months.

5.7 UNPAID LEAVE OF ABSENCE
-----------------------------
Unpaid leaves of absence may be approved at the company's discretion for
personal reasons, medical situations not covered by STD/FMLA, or sabbatical.
Requests should be submitted to HR at least 30 days in advance when possible.
Benefits continuation during unpaid leave requires COBRA enrollment.

6. WORK SCHEDULES AND REMOTE WORK
================================================================================

6.1 STANDARD WORK HOURS
------------------------
Core business hours are 9:00am to 5:00pm Central Time, Monday through Friday.
Most roles have flexibility around these hours provided business needs are met
and manager approval is obtained.

NOC roles operate on shift schedules. Shift schedules are published monthly
by the NOC Manager.

6.2 REMOTE WORK POLICY
------------------------
HYBRID ELIGIBLE ROLES: Most engineering and administrative roles are hybrid.
Hybrid employees work from the office a minimum of 2 days per week, typically
Tuesday and Thursday. Home office days are the employee's choice.

REMOTE ELIGIBLE ROLES: Certain individual contributor roles may be approved
as fully remote by VP approval. Remote employees located outside the Chicago
metro area must work within a US time zone compatible with CT business hours
(no earlier than ET).

ON-SITE REQUIRED ROLES: NOC staff, facilities, and certain infrastructure
roles requiring data center access are required to be on-site per their
schedule.

Remote work does not change the employee's on-call obligations. All on-call
engineers must maintain a reliable internet connection capable of VPN access
during on-call weeks.

6.3 HOME OFFICE STIPEND
-------------------------
Hybrid and fully remote employees receive a one-time home office setup stipend
of $1,000 upon hire, and a $500 annual refresh stipend. Equipment purchased
with the stipend belongs to the employee.

Nexus also provides a company-issued laptop (see Section 8) and pays for a
home internet stipend of $80/month for fully remote employees.

6.4 TRAVEL REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------
Some customer-facing roles and senior individual contributors require periodic
travel to customer sites or company locations. Expected travel frequency is
noted in the job description at time of hire.

Travel expenses are reimbursed per the Nexus Travel and Expense policy.
All travel must be booked through the Concur travel portal unless prior
approval is obtained. Mileage reimbursement rate: IRS standard rate (2026).

7. CODE OF CONDUCT AND PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS
================================================================================

7.1 PROFESSIONAL BEHAVIOR
--------------------------
All employees are expected to conduct themselves with professionalism,
integrity, and respect in all interactions with colleagues, customers, vendors,
and the public. This applies in person, on phone calls, via email, in Slack,
on social media, and at company events.

7.2 CUSTOMER INTERACTION STANDARDS
------------------------------------
When interacting with customers, employees represent the Nexus brand.
Standards include:
- Respond to customer communications within 1 business day
- Never make promises you cannot keep or commitments without manager approval
- Escalate customer complaints immediately rather than attempting to handle
  issues outside your authority
- Do not share confidential information about other customers
- Never speak negatively about competitors in front of customers

7.3 CONFLICT OF INTEREST
-------------------------
Employees must disclose any real or potential conflict of interest to HR
immediately. Conflicts include but are not limited to:
- Outside employment or consulting that competes with or involves Nexus
  customers
- Financial interest in a vendor, customer, or competitor
- Personal relationship with a direct report, customer, or vendor

Disclosure does not automatically result in discipline. Undisclosed conflicts
discovered after the fact may result in termination.

7.4 CONFIDENTIALITY AND NON-DISCLOSURE
----------------------------------------
All employees sign a Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure Agreement (CNDA) as
a condition of employment. Confidential information includes:
- Customer data, configurations, and contact information
- Company financial data
- Proprietary technical processes, code, and documentation
- Pricing, contracts, and business strategies
- Personnel information

Confidentiality obligations survive termination of employment.

7.5 HARASSMENT AND DISCRIMINATION
-----------------------------------
Nexus Technology Solutions has zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination,
or retaliation. Harassment includes verbal, physical, and visual conduct that
creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work environment.

Report harassment to:
- Your direct manager (if the manager is not the subject of the complaint)
- HR department: hr@nexustech.io or +1-312-555-0500
- Anonymous hotline: 1-800-555-SAFE (1-800-555-7233)

All reports are treated confidentially to the extent possible. Retaliation
against employees who report harassment in good faith is prohibited and may
result in immediate termination.

7.6 SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY
------------------------
Employees are free to use social media for personal purposes but must:
- Never share confidential company or customer information
- Not claim to speak on behalf of Nexus unless authorized by Marketing
- Not make defamatory statements about competitors, customers, or colleagues
- Be aware that public posts can affect Nexus' reputation

The Nexus LinkedIn company page is managed by the Marketing team. Employees
are encouraged to share approved company content but should not post original
content on behalf of Nexus without approval.

8. TECHNOLOGY AND SECURITY POLICIES
================================================================================

8.1 ACCEPTABLE USE OF COMPANY SYSTEMS
---------------------------------------
Company-provided systems (laptops, accounts, infrastructure access) are to be
used primarily for business purposes. Incidental personal use is permitted
provided it does not:
- Interfere with work responsibilities
- Involve illegal activity
- Involve downloading or installing unauthorized software
- Involve accessing inappropriate content

Nexus reserves the right to monitor activity on company-owned systems and
company networks. Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using
company systems.

8.2 ENDPOINT SECURITY REQUIREMENTS
------------------------------------
All company-issued laptops must have:
- CrowdStrike Falcon agent installed and running (auto-deployed at provisioning)
- Company MDM profile enrolled (Jamf for Mac, Intune for Windows)
- Full disk encryption enabled (FileVault on Mac, BitLocker on Windows)
- Screen lock set to activate after maximum 5 minutes of inactivity
- Automatic OS updates enabled

Employees may not disable, tamper with, or attempt to uninstall security
agents. Violations are treated as a serious security policy violation.

8.3 PASSWORD AND AUTHENTICATION REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------
- All Nexus accounts require MFA (Microsoft Authenticator preferred)
- Passwords must be at least 16 characters for privileged accounts,
  12 characters for standard accounts
- Passwords must not be reused within the last 12 cycles
- Use of a company-approved password manager (1Password) is mandatory for
  all employees — licenses are provided at no cost
- Never share passwords with colleagues or record passwords in plain text

8.4 VPN AND REMOTE ACCESS
--------------------------
All remote access to company systems must use GlobalProtect VPN. Exceptions
require CISO (Sandra Kim) approval.

Split-tunneling is disabled on the corporate VPN profile. All traffic from
company devices is routed through the Palo Alto firewall when VPN is active.

Customer system access (SSH, RDP, vSphere, etc.) must ONLY occur through
the CyberArk privileged access gateway. Direct connections to customer
environments are not permitted.

8.5 DATA HANDLING
------------------
CUSTOMER DATA CLASSIFICATION:
- Restricted: Customer PII, financial data, PHI — must be encrypted at rest
  and in transit, never stored on personal devices, deleted per retention
  schedules
- Confidential: Customer configurations, infrastructure details, passwords
  — encrypted in transit, access limited to need-to-know
- Internal: General business data — standard protection
- Public: Marketing materials, public documentation — no restrictions

Data must not be stored on personal devices (phones, personal laptops, USB
drives). Use company-approved storage only (OneDrive, SharePoint, or
company-approved cloud services).

8.6 SECURITY INCIDENT REPORTING
---------------------------------
Report security incidents IMMEDIATELY to the Security Operations Center:
- Email: soc@nexustech.io
- Phone: +1-312-555-0600 (24/7)
- Slack: #security-incidents

Examples of security incidents to report:
- Lost or stolen device
- Phishing email clicked
- Suspicious login notification
- Unknown software installed on your device
- Exposure of customer data

Do NOT attempt to investigate security incidents yourself. Notify the SOC
and follow their instructions.

9. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

9.1 PERFORMANCE REVIEW CYCLE
------------------------------
Nexus uses a twice-annual performance review cycle:
- Mid-year review: July (feedback and goal check-in, no rating)
- Annual review: December (full rating, compensation adjustment, bonus)

Reviews are completed in Workday. All employees and managers are required to
complete self-assessments before manager assessments are submitted.

9.2 PERFORMANCE RATINGS
------------------------
5 — Exceptional: Consistently exceeds all expectations. Clear top performer.
4 — Exceeds Expectations: Regularly goes above and beyond in most areas.
3 — Meets Expectations: Fully competent, reliably meets all commitments.
2 — Development Needed: Partially meets expectations; improvement required.
1 — Unsatisfactory: Consistently fails to meet basic expectations.

Distribution target: approximately 15% at 5, 25% at 4, 50% at 3, 10% at 2/1.
Distribution targets are guidelines, not quotas.

9.3 PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLANS
-----------------------------------
Employees receiving a rating of 2 may be placed on a Performance Improvement
Plan (PIP) by their manager with HR involvement. PIPs are 60-90 days in
duration and include:
- Specific, measurable improvement goals
- Defined support and resources from the manager
- Clear consequences if goals are not met

PIPs are intended as genuine improvement tools, not as a formality before
termination. Employees who successfully complete a PIP are removed from
probation and may be eligible for normal performance ratings.

10. SEPARATION OF EMPLOYMENT
================================================================================

10.1 VOLUNTARY RESIGNATION
---------------------------
Employees are requested to provide:
- Individual contributors and managers: 2 weeks written notice
- Senior managers and VP level: 4 weeks written notice

While notice is appreciated, at-will employment means neither party is
legally required to provide notice. Nexus may accept a resignation effective
immediately at its discretion.

10.2 INVOLUNTARY TERMINATION
------------------------------
Employees may be terminated for cause (policy violations, performance, conduct)
or without cause (layoff, restructuring). Terminated employees will receive
their final paycheck on the next regular payday or earlier as required by law.

Nexus does not offer severance as a standard practice. Severance may be offered
at the company's discretion in connection with layoffs or mutually agreed
separations, and would be documented in a separate separation agreement.

10.3 OFFBOARDING PROCESS
-------------------------
On or before the last day of employment, employees must:
- Return all company property: laptop, badge, peripherals, documents
- Provide system access and credential information if requested
- Complete exit interview with HR (voluntary but encouraged)
- Ensure work is documented and handed off to manager or designated colleague

IT will disable all access immediately upon separation. CyberArk will rotate
all managed credentials touched by the departing employee within 24 hours.

10.4 COBRA AND BENEFITS CONTINUATION
--------------------------------------
Health, dental, and vision benefits end on the last day of the month in which
employment ends. Employees are eligible for COBRA continuation coverage.
COBRA election information is mailed within 14 days of separation.
