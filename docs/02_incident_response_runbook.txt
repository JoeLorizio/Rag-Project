1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
================================================================================

This runbook defines the standard operating procedures for identifying,
classifying, escalating, and resolving incidents at Nexus Technology Solutions.
It applies to all production systems managed by the Infrastructure, Cloud, and
NOC teams, including customer-managed services.

All engineers are expected to be familiar with this document before joining an
on-call rotation. New hires must complete the Incident Response training module
in Workday Learning within 30 days of their start date.

This document should be treated as a living document. Any engineer may propose
changes via pull request to the internal GitLab repository (infra/runbooks).
Changes require approval from the Infrastructure Lead and NOC Manager.

2. INCIDENT SEVERITY DEFINITIONS
================================================================================

P1 — CRITICAL
--------------
Definition: Complete or near-complete loss of service for production systems
affecting customers or posing a risk of data loss or security breach.

Examples:
- Customer portal completely unavailable
- API gateway returning 5xx errors for more than 5% of requests for >5 minutes
- Database cluster unavailable or at risk of data loss
- Security breach confirmed or strongly suspected
- Ransomware or malware detected on production systems
- Core network failure affecting multiple customers

Response SLA: Acknowledge within 15 minutes, first status update within
30 minutes, resolution or workaround within 4 hours.

P2 — HIGH
----------
Definition: Partial service degradation affecting a subset of customers or
functionality, or a condition likely to escalate to P1 without intervention.

Examples:
- API latency above 3x normal baseline for >10 minutes
- Single-customer portal outage
- Backup job failures for more than 2 consecutive days
- Disk utilization above 85% on production systems
- Certificate expiration within 14 days on customer-facing endpoint
- Single node failure in a redundant cluster

Response SLA: Acknowledge within 1 hour, resolution or escalation within
8 hours during business hours, 12 hours off-hours.

P3 — MEDIUM
-----------
Definition: Non-customer-impacting issues, warning-level alerts, or
degraded performance not affecting SLAs.

Examples:
- Disk utilization 75-85%
- Non-production environment outages
- Single failed monitoring check with successful retry
- Minor configuration drift detected

Response SLA: Acknowledge within 4 business hours, resolve within 5
business days.

P4 — LOW
---------
Definition: Informational alerts, minor issues, improvement opportunities.

Response SLA: Logged and triaged at next team standup.

5. INCIDENT RESPONSE PROCEDURES BY SEVERITY
================================================================================

5.1 P1 INCIDENT PROCEDURE
--------------------------

STEP 1: DETECTION AND ACKNOWLEDGMENT (Target: 0-15 minutes)
- PagerDuty alert fires, primary on-call acknowledges
- Verify the alert is genuine (not a flapping monitor or false positive)
- If genuine P1 confirmed, immediately:
  a. Acknowledge PagerDuty alert
  b. Join #incidents Slack channel and announce: "I am investigating [alert
     name], acknowledged at [time]"
  c. Create incident ticket in ServiceNow (template: P1-TEMPLATE-001)
  d. Begin collecting initial data (see Section 7 for system-specific runbooks)

STEP 2: INITIAL ASSESSMENT (Target: 15-30 minutes)
- Determine blast radius: how many customers/services affected?
- Identify likely root cause area (network, compute, application, database)
- Page additional resources if needed (TL, IC, database lead, etc.)
- Post first status update to Statuspage.io (use template in Section 6)

STEP 3: INVESTIGATION AND MITIGATION (Target: 30-240 minutes)
- Technical Lead owns investigation
- Document all commands run, configuration changes, findings in ServiceNow
- Consider rollback as a mitigation if recent deployment is suspected
- Communicate status to IC every 15 minutes
- IC posts customer updates to Statuspage every 30 minutes

STEP 4: RESOLUTION
- Confirm service restoration via monitoring dashboards
- Verify customer-impacting symptoms resolved
- Post "resolved" update to Statuspage
- Send resolution email to affected customers (template in Section 6)
- Notify account management team

STEP 5: POST-INCIDENT HANDOFF
- Write initial incident summary in ServiceNow (due within 2 hours)
- Schedule post-incident review (PIR) within 5 business days
- Hand off to next on-call if incident spans shift change

5.2 P2 INCIDENT PROCEDURE
--------------------------

1. Acknowledge PagerDuty alert within 1 hour
2. Post in #alerts Slack channel: "Investigating [alert name] — [engineer name]"
3. Create ServiceNow ticket (P2-TEMPLATE-001)
4. Investigate and resolve or escalate to P1 if condition worsens
5. Document resolution in ServiceNow
6. No customer communication required unless SLA is at risk

5.3 P3 AND P4 INCIDENT PROCEDURE
----------------------------------
P3: Create ServiceNow ticket, assign to appropriate team queue, resolve within
5 business days. Discuss at next team standup.

P4: Log in ServiceNow, tag as P4, triage at next standup, no response SLA.

7. RUNBOOKS BY SYSTEM TYPE
================================================================================

7.1 KUBERNETES CLUSTER ISSUES
-------------------------------

SYMPTOM: Pods in CrashLoopBackOff
1. kubectl get pods -n [namespace] — identify affected pods
2. kubectl logs [pod-name] --previous — check logs from crashed instance
3. kubectl describe pod [pod-name] — check events and resource limits
4. Check Prometheus: query container_restart_count{namespace="[ns]"}
5. Common causes: OOM kill (increase memory limit), config error (check
   recent deployments in ArgoCD), dependency unavailable (check service
   dependencies)
6. If OOM: kubectl top pods -n [namespace] to confirm, scale vertically
7. If bad deployment: kubectl rollout undo deployment/[name] -n [namespace]

SYMPTOM: Node NotReady
1. kubectl get nodes — identify NotReady nodes
2. kubectl describe node [node-name] — check conditions and events
3. SSH to node: systemctl status kubelet
4. Check disk: df -h — nodes go NotReady when disk >85% on system partition
5. Check memory: free -h — check for OOM
6. If node is recoverable: sudo systemctl restart kubelet
7. If node hardware failure: cordon and drain (kubectl cordon / kubectl drain)

SYMPTOM: Kubernetes API server unreachable
1. Verify control plane nodes are running: check Grafana "Kubernetes Cluster"
   dashboard
2. SSH to control plane node: sudo crictl ps | grep kube-apiserver
3. Check etcd health: sudo etcdctl endpoint health --cluster
4. If etcd quorum lost: THIS IS A P1 — escalate to Infrastructure Lead
   immediately. Do NOT attempt etcd recovery without senior guidance.

7.2 DATABASE ISSUES
--------------------

SYMPTOM: PostgreSQL primary unavailable (Patroni cluster)
1. Check Patroni status: patronictl -c /etc/patroni.yml list
2. If automatic failover has occurred, Patroni promotes replica automatically
   — verify new primary is healthy
3. Check connection pool (PgBouncer): systemctl status pgbouncer
4. If failover did NOT occur: check primary server health via vSphere console
5. Manual failover (only with IC approval): patronictl failover nexus-pg-cluster
6. Verify application reconnection after failover — some apps require restart
7. Page James Okafor (Database Lead) for any P1 database incident

SYMPTOM: Database replication lag > 60 seconds
1. Check Patroni lag: patronictl -c /etc/patroni.yml list (shows lag in bytes)
2. Check replica I/O: vmstat 1 10 on replica server
3. Common cause: network congestion on storage VLAN (VLAN 40)
4. Check iSCSI traffic on Nexus switches: show interfaces ethernet [port]
5. If lag > 30 minutes: alert on-call DBA (James Okafor)
6. If lag > 2 hours: consider P1 escalation — RPO may be at risk

7.3 NETWORK ISSUES
-------------------

SYMPTOM: MPLS WAN circuit down
1. Check AT&T portal: att.com/business/network-management
2. Verify SD-WAN failover to internet backup occurred automatically
   — check Viptela dashboard
3. Check Palo Alto GlobalProtect VPN performance (may be degraded on internet)
4. Open AT&T ticket if circuit is confirmed down: 1-800-222-0300
5. Notify Marcus Chen and all ICs if MPLS is down > 30 minutes
6. Check if internet DIA circuits are healthy (Comcast: 1-800-391-3000)

SYMPTOM: Firewall policy blocking legitimate traffic
1. Check Palo Alto Traffic logs in Panorama
2. Filter by destination IP and application to identify block rule
3. Do NOT disable security policies without IC approval
4. If business-critical traffic is blocked: create temporary allow rule with
   expiration (24 hours max) and document in ServiceNow
5. Schedule proper rule review within 5 business days

7.4 STORAGE ISSUES
-------------------

SYMPTOM: PowerStore array alerts
1. Log into PowerStore Manager: https://powerstore-chi.nexustech.local
2. Check Events and Alerts section
3. For drive failure: array handles automatically if spare available
4. For multiple drive failures (degraded pool): P1 — call Dell EMC support
   immediately: 1-800-945-3355, reference tag NXT-PROD-2024
5. For capacity alert (>85%): identify largest datastores, escalate to IC
   if no immediate relief available

SYMPTOM: vSAN health degraded
1. Check vSAN Health in vSphere Client (Monitor > vSAN > Health)
2. Host network failure: check vSAN VMkernel adapters on affected host
3. Disk failure in vSAN: check vSAN disk groups, identify failed device
4. If vSAN is below required FTT (Failure to Tolerate) level: P1 immediately

8. POST-INCIDENT REVIEW PROCESS
================================================================================

8.1 PIR REQUIREMENTS
---------------------
All P1 incidents require a formal Post-Incident Review (PIR). P2 incidents
require a PIR if customer SLA was breached or if the IC determines one is
warranted.

PIR document must be completed within 5 business days of incident resolution.
PIR template is available in Confluence: https://nexustech.atlassian.net/pir

8.2 PIR STRUCTURE
------------------
Required sections:
1. Incident summary (1 paragraph)
2. Timeline of events (from first alert to resolution, with timestamps)
3. Root cause analysis (use 5 Whys methodology)
4. Customer impact statement (duration, scope, SLA credit if applicable)
5. Contributing factors (technical, process, human)
6. Action items (specific, assigned, with due dates)
7. Lessons learned

8.3 PIR DISTRIBUTION
---------------------
Completed PIR is reviewed in the weekly Infrastructure team meeting and
shared with:
- All infrastructure team members
- VP of Engineering
- Account management team (for any customer-impacting incidents)
- Customer success managers for directly affected customers

Blameless culture: PIRs focus on systems and processes, not individuals.
Engineers are encouraged to be transparent about mistakes. Blame or
punitive language is not permitted in PIR documents.

8.4 ACTION ITEM TRACKING
-------------------------
All PIR action items are tracked in ServiceNow as linked tasks to the
original incident. Due dates are mandatory. Overdue action items are
reviewed weekly by the Infrastructure Lead and escalated to the VP if
overdue by more than 2 weeks.
