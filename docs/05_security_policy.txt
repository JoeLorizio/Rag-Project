1. PURPOSE, SCOPE, AND OBJECTIVES
================================================================================

1.1 PURPOSE
-----------
This Information Security Policy establishes the security requirements,
standards, and guidelines that govern the protection of Nexus Technology
Solutions' information assets and the systems used to process, store, and
transmit those assets, including customer data entrusted to Nexus under
managed service agreements.

1.2 SCOPE
---------
This policy applies to:
- All Nexus Technology Solutions employees, contractors, and consultants
- All company-owned and managed systems, applications, and infrastructure
- All cloud-hosted systems managed by or on behalf of Nexus
- Customer systems managed under managed service agreements (MSAs)
- Third-party vendors with access to Nexus or customer systems

1.3 OBJECTIVES
--------------
- Protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information
- Comply with applicable legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements
- Prevent unauthorized access to systems and data
- Enable rapid detection and response to security incidents
- Maintain customer trust through demonstrable security practices

1.4 COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK
-------------------------
Nexus Technology Solutions maintains the following compliance certifications
and frameworks:

SOC 2 Type II: Annually audited (most recent: October 2025)
- Trust Service Criteria: Security, Availability, Confidentiality
- Auditor: Deloitte & Touche LLP
- Clean opinion issued November 2025
- Next audit: October 2026

HIPAA Business Associate: Nexus operates as a Business Associate for 8
healthcare customers. BAAs are on file for all 8. Annual HIPAA risk
assessment completed September 2025.

PCI DSS: Nexus processes no payment card data directly. Applicable to
customers; Nexus assists customers with PCI scoping and compliance.

ISO 27001: Pursuit planned for 2026. Pre-assessment completed Q3 2025.
Identified 34 gaps; remediation 60% complete.

NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) 2.0: Used as internal maturity model.
Current maturity assessment: 3.2/5.0 overall.

maintained by the GRC team in ServiceNow GRC module.

3. ACCESS CONTROL POLICY
================================================================================

3.1 PRINCIPLE OF LEAST PRIVILEGE
----------------------------------
All access rights must adhere to the principle of least privilege: users and
systems are granted only the access required to perform their function. Access
rights must be reviewed at least annually and upon any role change.

3.2 USER ACCESS MANAGEMENT
---------------------------
Provisioning:
- All user accounts are created through the IT service desk via ServiceNow
- Manager approval is required for all access requests
- Access is provisioned within 1 business day for standard access,
  3 business days for privileged access
- New employee accounts are pre-staged 24 hours before start date
- Access is removed within 4 hours of separation notification from HR

Review:
- Quarterly access reviews for privileged accounts and sensitive systems
- Annual access review for all other accounts
- Access recertification completed by managers in Saviynt IGA platform
- Accounts not recertified within the window are automatically disabled

Privileged Access:
- Privileged accounts (domain admin, root, vSphere admin) are managed in
  CyberArk PAM
- No direct login with privileged credentials — all privileged sessions
  use CyberArk's session proxy
- Privileged sessions are recorded and retained for 12 months
- Break-glass accounts are sealed in CyberArk with dual-approval workflow

3.3 AUTHENTICATION REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------
Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):
- Required for ALL access to company systems (no exceptions)
- Microsoft Authenticator (push notification or TOTP) is the standard
- FIDO2 hardware keys (YubiKey) are required for: all domain admin accounts,
  all CISO-level access, CI/CD pipeline service accounts with prod access
- SMS-based MFA is not permitted for any privileged access

Password Requirements (see also Section 8.3 of Employee Handbook):
- Minimum 16 characters for privileged accounts
- Minimum 12 characters for standard accounts
- No dictionary words, keyboard patterns, or personal information
- Enforce through Azure AD Password Protection and CyberArk policies
- Passphrase format strongly encouraged

Service Accounts:
- Must use certificate-based or API key authentication where possible
- If password required: minimum 32 characters, randomly generated,
  managed in CyberArk
- Service accounts must not have interactive login privileges
- Service accounts must not share credentials across multiple services

3.4 REMOTE ACCESS POLICY
-------------------------
- All remote access to Nexus systems requires GlobalProtect VPN
- VPN requires MFA (push notification, minimum)
- Split-tunneling is disabled on corporate VPN profiles
- VPN access for contractors is time-limited (90-day automatic expiration,
  renewable with manager approval)
- Customer system access exclusively via CyberArk session proxy
- Palo Alto Prisma Access (ZTNA) is being deployed for developer access
  to Kubernetes clusters, replacing SSH over VPN (target: Q2 2026)

4. DATA CLASSIFICATION AND HANDLING
================================================================================

4.1 DATA CLASSIFICATION LEVELS
--------------------------------
RESTRICTED (Highest sensitivity):
Definition: Data that if disclosed could result in significant legal, financial,
or reputational harm, or that is subject to regulatory requirements.
Examples: Customer PHI, PII (SSN, DOB, financial account numbers),
authentication credentials, cryptographic keys, security vulnerabilities,
M&A information, employee medical information
Handling: Encrypt at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+), access limited
to authorized personnel on need-to-know basis, no storage on unmanaged devices,
audit log all access, delete per retention schedule

CONFIDENTIAL:
Definition: Internal business information not approved for external distribution.
Examples: Customer configurations, internal pricing, personnel data (non-PHI),
business strategies, contract terms, incident reports, security policies
Handling: Encrypt in transit, store in company-approved systems, limit access
to employees with business need

INTERNAL:
Definition: General internal business information, limited risk if disclosed.
Examples: Internal process documents, meeting notes, training materials,
general IT documentation
Handling: Company network or approved cloud storage, basic access controls

PUBLIC:
Definition: Information approved for public release.
Examples: Marketing materials, public website content, published job postings
Handling: No special requirements

4.2 CUSTOMER DATA HANDLING
---------------------------
Customer data processed or stored by Nexus under an MSA is classified as
Restricted by default regardless of the customer's own classification.

Customer data must:
- Be stored only in systems and locations specified in the MSA
- Never be co-mingled with other customer data without explicit approval
- Be deleted within 30 days of contract termination per the data deletion
  addendum in the standard MSA
- Not be used for any purpose other than delivering the contracted service
- Not be accessed by Nexus personnel without documented business justification

Violations of customer data handling requirements are treated as P1 security
incidents and must be reported to the CISO within 1 hour of discovery.

4.3 DATA RETENTION AND DISPOSAL
---------------------------------
Standard retention schedules:
- Customer operational data: Duration of contract + 1 year
- Security logs and audit trails: 2 years minimum (7 years for HIPAA customers)
- Financial records: 7 years
- Email: 7 years (Microsoft 365 retention policy)
- Employee records: 7 years post-separation

Data disposal requirements:
- Electronic media: NIST 800-88 compliant wiping (Blancco for HDDs/SSDs)
- Physical media (paper): Cross-cut shredding via Iron Mountain shred service
- Cloud data: API-driven deletion with confirmation logs retained
- Data disposal is logged and certificates retained for 3 years

5. ENDPOINT SECURITY POLICY
================================================================================

5.1 ENDPOINT REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------
All company-managed endpoints (laptops, workstations) must have:

CrowdStrike Falcon Agent:
- Prevention policy: Block on severity 3+ detections
- Threat intelligence: Falcon Intelligence subscription
- Identity protection: Falcon Identity enabled for domain-joined systems
- USB device control: Restrict to company-approved USB devices
- Response: Real-time response (RTR) enabled for SOC use

MDM Enrollment:
- Jamf Pro for macOS (all Mac endpoints)
- Microsoft Intune for Windows (all Windows endpoints)
- Configuration profiles enforced: disk encryption, screen lock, firewall,
  software update policies
- Non-compliant devices blocked from network via Conditional Access

Approved Operating Systems:
- macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later — no exceptions
- Windows 11 22H2 or later — no exceptions
- Legacy OS devices must be isolated on separate VLAN and approved by CISO

5.2 SOFTWARE ALLOWLIST
-----------------------
Only approved software may be installed on company endpoints. Software requests
are submitted through ServiceNow and approved by IT and Security within
5 business days.

Pre-approved software categories (no ticket needed):
- Developer tools from approved vendors (see intranet list)
- Microsoft 365 applications
- Browser extensions reviewed and approved by security team

Prohibited software:
- Unapproved VPN clients
- Peer-to-peer file sharing applications
- Personal cloud storage clients (Google Drive, Dropbox personal — OneDrive
  for Business is approved)
- Software from torrent or unofficial sources
- Cryptocurrency mining software

5.3 MOBILE DEVICE POLICY
-------------------------
Personal mobile devices (BYOD) may access email and basic Microsoft 365
applications via Microsoft Authenticator + Intune App Protection Policy.

BYOD devices may NOT:
- Access Nexus internal network via VPN
- Access customer systems
- Store company data outside Microsoft 365 protected apps
- Be used for on-call response to production systems (must use company device)

Company-issued mobile phones are provided to: all NOC on-call staff,
account managers, VP and above. Managed via Intune.

6. NETWORK SECURITY POLICY
================================================================================

6.1 NETWORK SEGMENTATION
--------------------------
Nexus production networks are segmented per the VLAN structure documented
in the Infrastructure Overview. Traffic between segments is controlled by
Palo Alto firewalls with explicit allow rules.

Zero-trust principles applied:
- No implicit trust based on network location
- All inter-segment traffic is inspected by firewall application policies
- East-west traffic within Kubernetes is controlled by Istio service mesh
  and enforced at the pod level
- Database access: only application pods in approved namespaces may connect
  to database endpoints

6.2 FIREWALL CHANGE MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------
All firewall rule changes require:
- ServiceNow change request with business justification
- Peer review by a second network security engineer
- CISO approval for any rule affecting the DMZ or reducing security posture
- Implementation only during approved maintenance windows (unless P1)
- Post-implementation verification documented in the change record
- Temporary rules must have an expiration date (maximum 90 days)

Firewall rules are reviewed quarterly. Rules unused for 90 days are flagged
for removal. Business justification must be re-submitted or rules are removed.

6.3 WIRELESS SECURITY
----------------------
Corporate wireless (SSID: NexusCorp):
- WPA3-Enterprise authentication
- RADIUS authentication via Cisco ISE
- Corporate devices only; certificate-based authentication
- Clients placed on corporate VLAN 70

Guest wireless (SSID: NexusGuest):
- WPA2-PSK with rotating monthly password
- Completely isolated from corporate network
- Internet-only access, 10Mbps bandwidth limit per client
- Password available from front desk or IT service desk

IOT/Lab wireless (SSID: NexusLab — not broadcast):
- Dedicated VLAN for lab equipment
- No access to production networks
- Requires IT ticket to obtain credentials

7. CLOUD SECURITY POLICY
================================================================================

7.1 AZURE SECURITY STANDARDS
------------------------------
All Azure subscriptions must comply with the following baseline:

Identity and Access:
- Azure RBAC: Assign roles at resource group level, not subscription
- No "Owner" role assignments to individual users
- All privileged Azure roles through Privileged Identity Management (PIM)
  with just-in-time access (maximum 8-hour activation)
- Service principals must use Managed Identity where possible; if secret
  required, stored in Azure Key Vault

Network:
- All virtual networks use private endpoints for PaaS services
- No public IP addresses on virtual machines (use Bastion or VPN)
- Network Security Groups on all subnets, reviewed quarterly
- Azure DDoS Protection Standard on all production VNets

Monitoring and Logging:
- All subscriptions enrolled in Microsoft Defender for Cloud (Standard tier)
- Defender for Cloud score target: 85% (current: 78%)
- All diagnostic logs sent to central Log Analytics workspace
- Azure Activity Log retention: 90 days in Log Analytics, 2 years in archive

7.2 AWS SECURITY STANDARDS
---------------------------
AWS accounts must comply with AWS Foundational Security Best Practices
(automated assessment via AWS Security Hub):
- Root account MFA enabled
- CloudTrail enabled in all regions, logs sent to central S3 bucket
- GuardDuty enabled in all regions
- S3 public access block enabled by default on all buckets
- No access keys for root account
- EC2 instances use IMDSv2 only

8. INCIDENT RESPONSE SECURITY REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

8.1 SECURITY INCIDENT DEFINITION
----------------------------------
A security incident is any event that actually or potentially compromises the
confidentiality, integrity, or availability of Nexus or customer data, or
violates security policy. This includes but is not limited to:

Confirmed incidents:
- Unauthorized access to systems or data
- Malware infection on any endpoint or server
- Data exfiltration (confirmed or suspected)
- Ransomware
- Unauthorized configuration change to security controls
- Physical breach of data center

Suspected incidents (investigate immediately):
- Unusual login patterns or failed MFA attempts
- Unexplained network traffic
- Alerts from CrowdStrike, Microsoft Defender, or Palo Alto WildFire
- Employee reports of suspicious activity

8.2 INCIDENT RESPONSE REQUIREMENTS
-------------------------------------
Reporting:
All suspected security incidents must be reported to the SOC within 1 hour
of discovery, regardless of certainty. Contact:
- SOC hotline: +1-312-555-0600 (24/7)
- Email: soc@nexustech.io
- Slack: #security-incidents

Do NOT attempt to investigate security incidents yourself unless you are SOC
staff or are specifically directed by the CISO.

Preservation:
Do not power off or wipe affected systems without SOC authorization. Evidence
preservation is critical for investigation and potential legal proceedings.

Regulatory Notification:
- HIPAA breach: Affected individuals notified within 60 days; HHS within
  60 days (or annually for breaches < 500 individuals)
- SOC 2 breach: Customer notification within 72 hours
- State breach notification laws: As required by applicable state law
  (typically 30-72 hours)
- CISO and Legal counsel involved in all regulatory notification decisions

8.3 SECURITY INCIDENT SLAs
---------------------------
Per the incident response runbook (Section 9 of IR Runbook), security incidents
map to severity levels as follows:

P1 Security (15-minute response): Active ransomware/malware, data breach
  confirmed, unauthorized privileged access, critical system compromise

P2 Security (1-hour response): Suspected data exposure, failed intrusion
  attempt, malware on isolated endpoint, suspicious privileged activity

P3 Security (4-hour response): Policy violation (non-critical), phishing
  attempt (no click), vulnerability with working exploit in the wild

9. VULNERABILITY MANAGEMENT PROGRAM
================================================================================

9.1 SCANNING SCHEDULE
----------------------
External vulnerability scanning (Tenable Nessus / Tenable.io):
- Internet-facing systems: Weekly authenticated scans
- All internal systems: Monthly credentialed scans
- Pre-production scanning: Required before any major infrastructure change

Web application scanning:
- DAST scanning: Bi-weekly on all customer-facing web applications
- SAST: Integrated into CI/CD pipeline (SonarQube + Semgrep)
- Penetration testing: Annual external penetration test by third party
  (most recent: August 2025, conducted by NCC Group)

Container scanning:
- All container images scanned by Trivy in CI/CD pipeline
- Images with critical CVEs are blocked from deployment to production
- Images with high CVEs require security team approval

9.2 REMEDIATION SLAs
---------------------
CRITICAL (CVSS 9.0-10.0): Patch or mitigate within 15 days
HIGH (CVSS 7.0-8.9): Patch or mitigate within 30 days
MEDIUM (CVSS 4.0-6.9): Patch or mitigate within 90 days
LOW (CVSS 0.1-3.9): Patch during next scheduled maintenance window

Exceptions to remediation SLAs require:
- Written risk acceptance signed by CISO
- Compensating controls documented
- Review at next security steering committee meeting

Current patch compliance (as of December 2025):
- Critical: 96% within SLA (4 exceptions, all with approved compensating
  controls)
- High: 89% within SLA (11 open items in remediation)
- Medium: 84% within SLA (acceptable threshold: 80%)

9.3 PENETRATION TESTING
------------------------
Annual third-party penetration test scope includes:
- External network penetration test
- Web application assessment (all customer-facing applications)
- Social engineering assessment (phishing simulation)
- Optional: Physical security assessment (conducted every 3 years)

Most recent penetration test: August 2025 (NCC Group)
- Critical findings: 0
- High findings: 3 (all remediated by October 2025)
- Medium findings: 11 (9 remediated, 2 in progress)
- Low/Informational: 24

Next penetration test: Scheduled August 2026.

10.1 MANDATORY TRAINING
------------------------
All employees must complete the following security training:

Annual (due January 31 each year):
- Information Security Awareness — 60 minutes (KnowBe4)
- Phishing awareness and simulation training — ongoing quarterly simulations
- HIPAA awareness (required for all staff due to healthcare customer base)
- Data handling and privacy — 30 minutes

Upon hire (within 30 days):
- Security awareness onboarding module — 45 minutes
- Acceptable use policy acknowledgment
- CNDA signing

Role-specific (technical staff):
- Secure coding practices — annual (OWASP Top 10, 90 minutes)
- Cloud security fundamentals — annual for cloud team
- Incident response tabletop participation — annual for on-call engineers

10.2 PHISHING SIMULATION PROGRAM
----------------------------------
KnowBe4 is used for ongoing phishing simulation and security awareness:
- Monthly simulated phishing emails sent to all staff
- Employees who click simulated phishing links are enrolled in immediate
  remedial training (15 minutes)
- Click rate tracked monthly; company target: < 5% click rate
- Current click rate (December 2025): 3.8% (below target, improving)
- Employees with 3+ clicks in a calendar year: mandatory 1:1 with CISO

11. THIRD-PARTY AND VENDOR SECURITY
================================================================================

11.1 VENDOR RISK ASSESSMENT
-----------------------------
All vendors with access to Nexus or customer systems must complete a vendor
risk assessment before access is granted:

Tier 1 Vendors (access to Restricted data or production systems):
- Full security questionnaire (SIG Lite format)
- SOC 2 Type II report review (or equivalent)
- Annual reassessment
- Security addendum required in contract
- Examples: Cloud providers, MSP tools, security vendors, backup vendors

Tier 2 Vendors (access to Confidential data or internal systems):
- Abbreviated security questionnaire
- Annual reassessment
- Examples: Payroll processor, HR system, CRM

Tier 3 Vendors (no data access, software only):
- Standard due diligence (company reputation, license terms)
- No formal questionnaire required

11.2 VENDOR ACCESS CONTROLS
-----------------------------
Vendor access to production systems:
- All vendor access via CyberArk (no direct credentials)
- Access scoped to minimum necessary systems
- Time-limited access: granted for defined project duration
- Sessions recorded and retained
- Access revoked within 24 hours of project completion
- Quarterly review of all active vendor access

11.3 SOFTWARE SUPPLY CHAIN
---------------------------
Open source and third-party software used in Nexus products:
- All open source dependencies scanned by Dependency-Track
- SBOM (Software Bill of Materials) generated for all production applications
- Critical dependencies reviewed quarterly by Security Engineering
- License compliance enforced (no GPL 3.0 in customer-deployed code without
  legal review)
